Hamilton-Jacobi Theory as a Local Projection of Boundary-Kernel Composition

A non-invertibility theorem from global gluing sectors

Version 0.2 · May 29, 2026 · manuscript

This manuscript clarifies the relation between boundary-kernel composition and Hamilton-Jacobi theory. It shows that composable boundary kernels can project to local Hamilton-Jacobi data in regular semiclassical regimes, while the projection is not invertible.

On a configuration space with non-contractible loops, distinct character-labelled globally composable kernels can have the same local Hamilton-Jacobi projection but different global composition data. The particle on a ring, equivalently the Aharonov-Bohm example, gives the standard concrete realization.
