Mathematical physics · foundations of physics

Roy Rosemarin

I am an independent researcher with a PhD in Statistics from the London School of Economics and a professional background in data science, machine learning, AI, and engineering systems. My current work is theoretical research on foundations of physics, exploring predictive-state structure, region composition, information geometry, and observer-dependent temporal descriptions.

Overview

Motivating Questions

The central motivation is to replace a state-at-an-instant view of physics with a boundary-compositional view. Instead of asking only how a state evolves through time, I ask what boundary data are required for fragments of a physical description to compose consistently across a cut, and what new variables are forced when that composition fails.

Current Directions

The present focus is cut-objective boundary-kernel theory: composable region weights and amplitudes, minimal boundary completions, sharp-limit response geometry, and the global gluing data that can be invisible to local Hamilton-Jacobi dynamics.

Open Problems

Longer-term questions include existence and uniqueness of minimal boundary completions, nonsmooth and multibranch sharp limits, observable-relative boundary algebras, quantum amplitude and record composition, hidden thermodynamic and causal boundary sectors, and geometric or constrained-system completions in more general physical settings.
