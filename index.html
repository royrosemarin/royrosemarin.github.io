Mathematical physics · foundations of physics

Roy Rosemarin

I am an independent researcher with a PhD in Statistics from the London School of Economics and a professional background in data science, machine learning, AI, and engineering systems. My current work is theoretical research on foundations of physics, exploring predictive-state structure, region composition, information geometry, and observer-dependent temporal descriptions.

Overview

Motivating Questions

The central motivation is to replace a state-at-an-instant view of physics with a boundary-compositional view. Instead of asking only how a state evolves through time, I ask what data must live on a cut so that history fragments compose into a cut-independent whole.

Current Directions

The present focus is cut-objective predictive mechanics: region weights, auxiliary cuts, complete boundary data, sharp generators, and the boundary-response geometry that appears in regular finite-dimensional limits.

Open Problems

Longer-term questions include existence and uniqueness of minimal predictive boundaries, nonsmooth and multibranch sharp limits, constrained or gauge boundary data, quantum amplitude composition, causal reconstruction from boundary sensitivity, and whether gravitational entropy can be understood through horizon boundary data rather than volume degrees of freedom.
