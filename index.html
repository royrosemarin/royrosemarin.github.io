Roy Rosemarin

I am an independent researcher with a PhD in Statistics from the London School of Economics. I explore predictive-state structure, region composition, information geometry, and observer-dependent temporal descriptions.

Overview

Motivating Questions

What has to be exposed on a boundary so that two descriptions of adjacent regions can be recomposed without changing the physical weight of the whole? Can phase-space response variables be reconstructed from this compositional requirement rather than assumed at the start?

Current Directions

The present focus is cut-objective predictive mechanics: region weights, auxiliary cuts, complete boundary data, sharp generators, and the boundary-response geometry that appears in regular finite-dimensional limits.

Open Problems

Longer-term questions include existence and uniqueness of minimal predictive boundaries, nonsmooth and multibranch sharp limits, constrained or gauge boundary data, quantum amplitude composition, causal reconstruction, and the relation between predictive gluing and temporal description.
